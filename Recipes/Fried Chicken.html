Fried Chicken Recipe

Recipe by Henry Higuan

Description

Fried chicken is a culinary delight that tantalizes the taste buds with its irresistible combination of crispy exterior and juicy, tender meat. The dish typically begins with marinating chicken pieces in a flavorful blend of herbs and spices, enhancing the overall taste experience. Once prepared, the chicken is coated in a seasoned batter or dredged in flour, creating a golden-brown crust when deep-fried or pan-fried to perfection.

The magic of fried chicken lies in its contrasting textures—a crunchy and savory outer layer that gives way to succulent, moist meat within. The aroma of spices and the sound of the sizzle as the chicken cooks add to the sensory appeal of this beloved dish. Accompanied by various dipping sauces, fried chicken offers a symphony of flavors, ranging from savory and salty to slightly sweet, satisfying cravings for comfort food. Whether enjoyed as a finger-licking snack, a centerpiece for a family meal, or a nostalgic treat at gatherings, fried chicken holds a special place in culinary culture, bringing people together through the simple joy of indulging in its delicious and satisfying goodness.

Recipe

  • 2-3 lbs (about 1-1.5 kg) chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, wings, etc.)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Procedure

  1. Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl.
  2. Pour the buttermilk over the chicken, making sure each piece is well-coated.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This helps tenderize the chicken and adds flavor.
  4. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, and cayenne pepper. Mix well to create a seasoned flour mixture.
  5. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk marinade, allowing any excess liquid to drip off.
  6. Dredge each piece in the seasoned flour mixture, ensuring an even coating. Press the flour onto the chicken to help it adhere.
  7. In a large, deep skillet or pot, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat to around 350-375°F (175-190°C). Use a thermometer to monitor the oil temperature.
  8. Carefully place a few pieces of chicken into the hot oil, being cautious not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and has an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
  9. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried chicken from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  10. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving.
  11. Serve the crispy fried chicken hot with your favorite sides, such as coleslaw, mashed potatoes, or biscuits.
